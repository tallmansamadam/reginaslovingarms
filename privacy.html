Privacy Policy & Notice of Privacy Practices

Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Last Updated: January 1, 2026 Regina's Loving Arms Home Health Care, LLC
This document is both our Privacy Policy and our Notice of Privacy Practices as required by HIPAA. It describes how Regina's Loving Arms Home Health Care, LLC collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards your personal information — including Protected Health Information (PHI) — in connection with the delivery of our home health care services and the use of this website. Please read it carefully. If you have questions, contact our Privacy Officer at 312-773-5553.

1. Who We Are

Regina's Loving Arms Home Health Care, LLC ("Company," "we," "us," or "our") is a licensed home health care agency located at 2020 N Lincoln Park West, Suite 18H, Chicago, IL 60614. We provide home health aide, live-in care, companion care, and related health and personal care services to clients in the Chicago metropolitan area.

As a provider of health care services and a covered entity under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA"), we are legally required to maintain the privacy and security of your Protected Health Information, to notify you of our legal duties and privacy practices, and to abide by the terms of this Notice currently in effect.

2. Information We Collect

We collect personal and health-related information necessary to provide, coordinate, and improve our services. This may include:

  • Contact & Identity Information: Full name, mailing address, telephone numbers, email address, date of birth, gender, and emergency contact details.
  • Protected Health Information (PHI): Medical history, diagnoses, treatment plans, medications, physician orders, functional limitations, insurance information, and any other health-related data required to plan and deliver care.
  • Payment & Insurance Information: Medicare or Medicaid beneficiary identifiers, private insurance policy numbers, billing and payment records, and Social Security numbers where required for billing purposes.
  • Employment Information (Applicants): Resume, credentials, licensure information, references, and background check data collected from prospective employees and contractors.
  • Website Usage Data: IP address, browser type and version, pages visited, time on page, and referring URLs collected automatically via cookies and similar tracking technologies for analytics and site improvement purposes.
  • Voluntary Communications: Any information you submit through our contact form, email correspondence, telephone calls, or in-person interactions with our staff.

We collect information directly from you and your authorized representatives, from referring physicians and hospitals, from government program administrators, and — with your authorization — from other treating providers.

3. How We Use Your Information

Under HIPAA, we are permitted to use and disclose your PHI without additional authorization for the following core purposes:

  • Treatment: To coordinate, provide, manage, and continuously improve the care and services delivered to you or your family member, including communication with other members of your care team.
  • Payment: To verify insurance eligibility, submit claims, obtain payment, and manage billing for services rendered to you through Medicare, Medicaid, private insurers, or private-pay arrangements.
  • Healthcare Operations: For internal quality assessment and improvement, staff training and supervision, legal and regulatory compliance, accreditation activities, and overall business management.
  • Care Coordination & Communications: To respond to your inquiries, schedule and confirm services, send appointment reminders, and communicate about your care plan.
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable federal and Illinois state laws, regulations, subpoenas, court orders, and mandatory reporting obligations.
  • Safety: To prevent or lessen a serious and imminent threat to the health or safety of you, another person, or the general public, in accordance with applicable law and ethical standards.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information or PHI to any third party for marketing purposes. Any use or disclosure of PHI beyond those described above requires your prior written authorization.

4. Your HIPAA Rights Regarding Your Health Information

As an individual whose PHI we hold, you have the following rights under HIPAA. To exercise any of these rights, please submit a written request to our Privacy Officer using the contact information in Section 11.

Right to Access

You may request an inspection and copy of your health records in electronic or paper form. We will respond within 30 days. A reasonable fee may apply for copies.

Right to Amend

You may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete PHI. We may deny the request under certain circumstances but will document your request in the record.

Right to an Accounting

You may request a list of certain disclosures we have made of your PHI, excluding disclosures for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations.

Right to Request Restrictions

You may ask us to limit how we use or disclose your PHI. We are not required to agree to all restrictions but will honor reasonable requests and all legally mandated restrictions.

Right to Confidential Communications

You may request that we contact you by a specific method or at a specific location (e.g., only by mail to a designated address). We will accommodate all reasonable requests.

Right to a Copy of This Notice

You may request a paper copy of this Notice of Privacy Practices at any time, regardless of whether you have agreed to receive this Notice electronically.

Right to Opt Out of Fundraising

If we ever use your contact information for fundraising purposes, you will have the right to opt out of receiving such communications at any time.

Right to File a Complaint

You may file a complaint with us or directly with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services Office for Civil Rights. We will never retaliate against you for filing a complaint.

5. Permitted Disclosures of PHI

In addition to the core uses described above, HIPAA and Illinois law permit us to disclose your PHI without your written authorization in the following circumstances:

  • To other healthcare providers directly involved in your treatment, including physicians, specialists, hospitals, pharmacies, and therapists.
  • To insurance companies, Medicare, Medicaid, or other payers for the purposes of payment processing and claims adjudication.
  • As required by applicable federal or Illinois state law, including mandatory reporting to public health authorities, child protective services, or adult protective services.
  • To public health authorities for disease surveillance, reporting of adverse events, and product safety recalls, as permitted or required by law.
  • To law enforcement officials in response to a lawful court order, warrant, subpoena, or as otherwise required or permitted by applicable law.
  • To avert a serious and imminent threat to the health or safety of you or another person, consistent with applicable law and professional ethical standards.
  • For oversight activities, including government audits, inspections, and investigations relating to the health care system or government benefit programs.
  • To our Business Associates — vendors and contractors who perform functions on our behalf — who are contractually bound by HIPAA-compliant Business Associate Agreements to protect your PHI.
  • For the purposes of organ and tissue donation, military and veterans' activities, correctional institution activities, or research conducted under appropriate privacy protections, as permitted by HIPAA.

Any use or disclosure of your PHI that is not described in this Notice requires your specific written authorization. You may revoke a written authorization at any time by submitting a written revocation to our Privacy Officer, except where we have already acted in reliance upon the authorization.

6. Illinois Law — Additional Protections

In addition to federal HIPAA requirements, we comply with all applicable Illinois state laws that provide equal or greater privacy protections. Key state law protections include:

  • Illinois Personal Information Protection Act (815 ILCS 530): We implement commercially reasonable security measures to protect your personal information. In the event of a data breach involving your unencrypted personal information, we will notify you in the manner and within the timeframe required by Illinois law.
  • Illinois Mental Health and Developmental Disabilities Confidentiality Act (740 ILCS 110): Mental health records and information are afforded the highest level of confidentiality protection under Illinois law and will not be disclosed without your specific written consent, except in the limited circumstances permitted by statute.
  • Illinois AIDS Confidentiality Act (410 ILCS 305): HIV/AIDS status and related records are strictly confidential and will only be disclosed with your express written consent or as expressly permitted by this statute.
  • Illinois Genetic Information Privacy Act (410 ILCS 513): Genetic testing results and related information are protected and will not be disclosed without your written consent except as required by law.
  • Illinois Home Health, Home Services, and Home Nursing Agency Licensing Act (210 ILCS 55): As a licensed home health agency in Illinois, we are subject to ongoing state oversight and must maintain records in accordance with state licensing requirements.

In cases where Illinois state law affords you greater privacy protection than HIPAA, we will follow the more protective state standard.

7. Data Security

We are committed to protecting your personal information and PHI through a comprehensive set of administrative, physical, and technical safeguards, as required by HIPAA's Security Rule (45 CFR Parts 160 and 164).

Our security measures include, but are not limited to:

  • Role-based access controls limiting PHI access to workforce members with a legitimate need to know.
  • Encryption of PHI transmitted electronically over open networks.
  • Workforce training on privacy and security obligations upon hire and at least annually thereafter.
  • Regular risk assessments and vulnerability analyses of our information systems.
  • Documented security incident response and breach notification procedures.
  • Physical safeguards for facilities and equipment containing PHI, including locked file storage and visitor access controls.
  • Business Associate Agreements with all vendors and contractors who access PHI on our behalf.

Despite our best efforts, no method of data transmission or storage is completely secure. In the event of a breach of unsecured PHI, we will notify affected individuals and the appropriate government authorities as required by the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR §§ 164.400–414) and applicable Illinois law.

8. Website Cookies & Analytics

Our website may use cookies and similar tracking technologies (such as web beacons and pixel tags) to analyze site traffic, understand how visitors interact with our pages, and improve the overall user experience.

Website usage data collected through cookies is not Protected Health Information and is handled separately from your clinical or care-related information. We do not use website cookies to collect or process PHI.

You may disable cookies at any time through your web browser settings. Disabling cookies will not affect your ability to contact us, receive care services, or exercise any of your privacy rights. However, some features of the website may not function optimally if cookies are disabled.

We do not sell or share website analytics data with third parties for their independent marketing purposes. Any third-party analytics providers we use (such as Google Analytics) process data pursuant to their own privacy policies and our data processing agreements with them.

9. Children's Privacy

This website is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 directly through this website. Care services for minor clients are delivered pursuant to the written authorization and informed consent of a parent or legal guardian, who will be provided with a copy of this Notice and will exercise HIPAA rights on behalf of the minor client in accordance with Illinois law.

If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a child under 13 through our website without appropriate authorization, please contact our Privacy Officer immediately at 312-773-5553 or info@reginaslovingarms.com.

10. Changes to This Notice

We reserve the right to change the terms of this Privacy Policy and Notice of Privacy Practices at any time. Any material changes will apply to PHI we already hold, as well as to information we receive in the future. When we make changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this Notice;
  • Post the revised Notice on this website;
  • Provide a copy of the revised Notice to active clients upon request; and
  • Where required by applicable law, provide direct notification of material changes.

Your continued receipt of services following the posting of a revised Notice constitutes your acknowledgment of the updated terms. You may request the current version of this Notice at any time by contacting our Privacy Officer.
