Personal Care

Caregiver Services

Our trained caregivers assist with the daily activities of life that can become challenging due to age, illness, or disability — preserving independence while ensuring safety and comfort. We carefully match every caregiver to each client based on personality, experience, and schedule, building relationships grounded in genuine trust and affection.

  • Bathing, grooming, and personal hygiene
  • Dressing and mobility assistance
  • Meal planning and preparation
  • Light housekeeping and laundry
  • Grocery shopping and errand running
  • Transportation to appointments
  • Medication reminders
  • Companionship and social engagement

Companion Care

Companion Care

Loneliness and social isolation are among the most significant — and most overlooked — health risks for older adults. Our companion care services address this directly, pairing clients with warm, engaging companions who provide meaningful social interaction, cognitive stimulation, and the simple gift of being present. For many clients, their companion becomes one of the most important relationships in their life.

  • Meaningful conversation and social engagement
  • Reading, games, and cognitive activities
  • Accompaniment to community events
  • Support for clients with memory challenges
  • Supervised leisure and hobby activities
  • Emotional support and active listening
  • Family communication and updates
  • Respite support for family caregivers

Around the Clock

24-Hour & Live-In Care

Some clients require continuous supervision and assistance — whether due to advanced dementia, high fall risk, post-acute recovery, or complex multi-system illness. Our 24-hour and live-in care programs ensure that a qualified caregiver or nurse is always present, providing families with the confidence that their loved one is never alone and always supported, day or night.

  • Continuous overnight supervision
  • Live-in caregiver placement
  • Rotating care team for shift-based coverage
  • Advanced dementia and Alzheimer's care
  • High fall-risk monitoring and prevention
  • Nighttime toileting and repositioning
  • Emergency response and crisis support
  • Family communication and daily care logs
